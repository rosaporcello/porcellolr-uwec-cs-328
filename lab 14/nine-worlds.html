The Nine Worlds & Yggdrasil

Völuspá

Stanzas 1–5

Hearing I ask | from the holy races,
From Heimdall's sons, | both high and low;
Thou wilt, Valfather, | that well I relate
Old tales I remember | of men long ago.

I remember yet | the giants of yore,
Who gave me bread | in the days gone by;
Nine worlds I knew, | the nine in the tree
With mighty roots | beneath the mold.

Of old was the age | when Ymir lived;
Sea nor cool waves | nor sand there were;
Earth had not been, | nor heaven above,
But a yawning gap, | and grass nowhere.

Then Bur's sons lifted | the level land,
Mithgarth the mighty | there they made;
The sun from the south | warmed the stones of earth,
And green was the ground | with growing leeks.

The sun, the sister | of the moon, from the south
Her right hand cast | over heaven's rim;
No knowledge she had | where her home should be,
The moon knew not | what might was his,
The stars knew not | where their stations were.

Yggdrasil

Yggdrasil is a tree central to the Norse concept of the cosmos. The tree's branches extend into various realms, and various creatures dwell on and around it. The gods go to Yggdrasil daily to assemble at their things, traditional governing assemblies. The branches of Yggdrasil extend far into the heavens, and the tree is supported by three roots that extend far away into other locations; one to the well Urðr in the heavens, one to the spring Hvergelmir, and another to the well Mímisbrunnr. Creatures live within Yggdrasil, including the dragon Níðhöggr, an unnamed eagle, and the stags Dáinn, Dvalinn, Duneyrr, and Duraþrór.

Asgard

Asgard is one of the Nine Realms and home to the Æsir gods. It is surrounded by an incomplete wall attributed to a Hrimthurs riding the stallion Svaðilfari. Odin and Frigg rule Asgard, and the realm is connected to Midgard by the Bifröst bridge.

Vanaheimr

Vanaheimr is the home of the Vanir gods, a family associated with fertility, wisdom, and foresight. The Vanir once warred with the Æsir before forming a truce, exchanging hostages, and eventually uniting their powers.

Alfheim

Alfheimr, the “elf home,” is ruled by Freyr and is home to the Light Elves. These beings are described as fairer than the sun and often connected to nature, magic, and beauty.

Midgard

Midgard, meaning “middle enclosure,” is the realm of humans. It is surrounded by a vast ocean inhabited by the Midgard Serpent, Jörmungandr. Midgard lies between realms of ice and fire and is connected to Asgard by the Bifröst.

Jötunheimr

Jötunheimr is home to the giants, ancient beings who frequently clash with gods and humans. The river Ífing separates Jötunheimr from Asgard. It contains strongholds of giants such as Útgarðr and Gastropnir.

Muspelheim

Muspelheim is the realm of fire, home to fire giants led by Surtr. The heat of Muspelheim once mixed with the frost of Niflheim to create the primordial giant Ymir. Surtr will lead the forces of fire during Ragnarök.

Svartálfar

Svartálfar, or “black elves,” dwell in Svartálfaheim. They are commonly associated with dwarves—master craftsmen who forged legendary items such as Thor’s hammer Mjölnir and Odin’s spear Gungnir.

Niflheim

Niflheim is a frigid realm of mist and ice. It is the land of the dishonored dead, ruled by Hel, daughter of Loki. The dragon Níðhöggr gnaws at the roots of Yggdrasil here.

Nidavellir

Nidavellir is the realm of the dwarves, skilled smiths who crafted powerful artifacts for the gods. It is described as a dark, underground land filled with forges and great halls.
